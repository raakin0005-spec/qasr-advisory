Frequently asked questions

Direct answers about university admissions consulting.

Common questions from international students and families considering undergraduate, master’s and long-term admissions support.

Working with an admissions consultant

What does a university admissions consultant do?

A university admissions consultant helps students plan a suitable university list, application strategy, timeline, academic and extracurricular profile, written materials, interviews and final decisions.

Who is Qasr Advisory for?

Qasr works with undergraduate, transfer and master’s applicants, including students who are actively applying and younger students who want a longer-term profile-building plan.

Does Qasr work with students in Saudi Arabia, Qatar and the UAE?

Yes. Qasr provides private online admissions consulting for students and families across Saudi Arabia, Qatar, the UAE and the wider GCC, as well as applicants elsewhere in the world.

Are consultations online?

Yes. Consultations and ongoing advisory can be delivered online, allowing students and parents in different cities or countries to participate.

When should university application planning begin?

Most students benefit from beginning 12 to 24 months before university enrolment. Focused support is also available for applicants already in an active admissions cycle.

Applications, destinations and curricula

Does Qasr help with Common App applications?

Yes. Common App support can include college selection, application positioning, activities, written sections, university supplements, school documents, recommendations and deadline planning.

Does Qasr help with UCAS applications?

Yes. UCAS support can include UK course and university selection, entry-requirement research, academic positioning, personal statements, references and interview preparation where required.

Which countries does Qasr cover?

Qasr advises families considering universities in the United States, United Kingdom, Europe, Canada, Australia, Singapore, Malaysia, Turkey, China, Qatar, the UAE and other international destinations.

Can Qasr advise students following IB, A levels, AP or an American curriculum?

Yes. Application strategy is adapted to the student’s actual curriculum, school context, predicted or achieved grades and the requirements of each target university.

Can Qasr help a student choose between the US, UK and Europe?

Yes. The comparison can consider degree structure, academic flexibility, admissions style, total cost, location, student experience and longer-term goals.

Undergraduate, master’s and profile support

Does Qasr support undergraduate applications?

Yes. Undergraduate support can cover university selection, Common App, UCAS, direct applications, activities, written materials, interviews and offer decisions.

Does Qasr support master’s applications?

Yes. Master’s admissions support can include program selection, application strategy, statements of purpose, CV development, recommenders, scholarships and interview preparation.

What is student profile building?

Profile building is structured long-term planning across academics, activities, leadership, research, internships, summer opportunities and exploration of genuine academic interests.

Can Qasr help with application essays and personal statements?

Yes. Qasr provides strategic guidance on topic selection, structure, clarity, reflection and how written materials connect with the wider application.

Does Qasr guarantee admission to a university?

No legitimate advisor can guarantee an admissions decision. Qasr helps students develop thoughtful, accurate and well-positioned applications while universities retain full control of their decisions.

Fees and getting started

How much does university admissions consulting cost?

The cost depends on the applicant’s timeline, number of universities and level of support. Qasr confirms the appropriate service and quote after an initial consultation.

How do I begin working with Qasr Advisory?

Complete the consultation form or contact Qasr on WhatsApp. The first conversation reviews the student’s background, goals, countries under consideration, deadlines and support needs.
