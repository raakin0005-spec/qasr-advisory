What does a master’s admissions consultant help with?

Qasr helps applicants choose suitable programs, position their academic and professional experience, coordinate written materials and recommendations, manage requirements and prepare for interviews.

A strategy specific to postgraduate study

Master’s admissions decisions depend on the relationship between the applicant’s preparation, the proposed field and the purpose of the degree. The application should make that relationship easy to understand.

Program and university selection

Compare curriculum, faculty strengths, admission requirements, career relevance, cost and location before finalising a balanced list.

Statement of purpose and application narrative

Develop a clear account of academic preparation, professional experience, present interests and the reason each program supports the applicant’s goals.

CV and experience positioning

Organise education, work, research, projects, leadership and relevant achievements so reviewers can quickly understand the applicant’s preparation.

Recommendations and requirements

Plan appropriate recommenders, timelines, transcripts, testing, portfolios and program-specific documents across every application.

Scholarship and interview preparation

Identify relevant funding opportunities and prepare concise, evidence-based answers for admissions or scholarship interviews.

Recent graduates

Working professionals

Explain how experience has shaped the need for advanced study and the next career step.

Career changers

Present transferable preparation and a realistic bridge into a new academic or professional field.
