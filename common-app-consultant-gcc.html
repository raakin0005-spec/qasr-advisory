What does a Common App consultant do?

A Common App consultant helps a student connect college selection, application positioning, activities, written responses, recommendations, testing and deadlines into one coherent US admissions strategy. The work should strengthen the student’s authentic presentation rather than manufacture a persona.

How the Common Application works for international students

The Common App allows a student to enter core information once and submit it to participating colleges. Each institution may still require its own questions, supplements, portfolio, financial documents or testing choices. International applicants also need to coordinate transcripts, school reports, recommendations and English-language requirements where applicable.

Students from Saudi Arabia, Qatar, the UAE and other GCC countries can use the platform. The application must make the student’s curriculum and context understandable to readers who may not know every school or national system.

Build a balanced US college list

We begin with academic interests, curriculum, grades, testing, family budget, campus preferences and future goals. A balanced list includes institutions with different levels of selectivity, but every option should be one the student would genuinely consider. Published admission rates alone do not predict an individual result.

Present activities with clarity and evidence

The activities section has limited space. Strong entries explain the student’s role, contribution, initiative and scope using specific language. Depth and sustained involvement usually communicate more than a collection of unrelated short-term activities.

Develop written materials around the student

The main response and university supplements should help admissions readers understand how the student thinks, what has shaped them and what they may contribute. Qasr supports topic development, structure, reflection, revision and application-wide consistency while keeping the student’s ideas and voice central.

Coordinate recommendations, testing and deadlines

A strong application also depends on early recommender planning, accurate school information, a sensible testing decision and careful deadline management. We create a requirements tracker so the family can see what is complete, what is pending and who owns each next step.

Review before submission

Final review checks accuracy, consistency, missing requirements and whether the application presents the student as clearly as possible. It is not a promise of admission; selective US admissions remain uncertain even for excellent applicants.

Common App questions from GCC families

Do Saudi students use the Common App?

Yes. Saudi students can apply through the Common App to participating US institutions and complete any university-specific or international-applicant requirements.

Is the Common App the same as UCAS?

No. Common App is widely used for US undergraduate admissions and evaluates a broad application. UCAS is used for UK undergraduate courses and places greater emphasis on academic course fit. Read our UCAS guide and consulting page.

When should we start?

College research and profile planning can begin well before Grade 12. Active application preparation is usually strongest when started months before the first deadline.

Does every US university use the Common App?

No. Many do, but some use another platform or their own application. Requirements must be confirmed on each university’s official admissions website.
